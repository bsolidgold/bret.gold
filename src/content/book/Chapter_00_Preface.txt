Preface

Here's the thing about minds like ours: they don't stay quiet. They don't follow rules. They don't respect the boundaries between past and present, between what happened and what might have happened, between what we can bear to remember and what we can't stop remembering.

Your brain is already doing it, isn't it? Even as you read these words, there's another voice in there — the one that never stops, the one that knows where all your bruises are, the one that remembers everything you wish you could forget. The one that's about to remind you of that thing you did, that person you lost, that moment when everything shifted and you didn't move fast enough to catch it.

*»You know exactly which voice I mean, don't you? The one that's been talking since before you opened this book.«*

I wish someone had told me it wasn't just me. That we all carry this thing — this witness, this keeper of every moment we've tried to bury. That it's not madness or weakness or failure. It's just what minds do when they've been through some shit and are trying to make sense of it.

That voice isn't me. That voice is you. I'm just the one writing it down.

I should warn you about something. My brain has this protector — this analytical part that's kept me alive by making sure nothing ever hits without a buffer. It's smart. It sounds like it has things figured out. When this book starts over-explaining, when it gets too philosophical, when it starts turning pain into a thesis statement — that's him. That's the part of me that would rather dissect a feeling than actually feel it. He's been running the show for a long time and he's not going to stop just because I'm writing a book. So he's coming along for the ride. But I'll try to keep him honest.

The rest of it — the raw parts, the ugly parts, the parts that don't make sense and aren't wrapped up in a nice little bow — that's the other voice. The one that doesn't have words half the time. The one whose mouth doesn't speak the same language as his brain. That's the one telling the truth. You'll learn to tell the difference.

This isn't a guide. There are no five steps to healing, no map through the territory of your own mind. I'm not going to tell you it gets better because I don't know if it does. I'm not going to inspire you because that's not what this is. This is just one person saying: I've been in that dark place. I'm probably still in it. And I'm going to tell you what it actually looks like in here — not the cleaned-up version, not the version that makes for a good Instagram caption, but the version with the mess still on the floor.

If that makes any sense.
